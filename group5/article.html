Emerging Treatments and Tech

Rise of cognitive assistive technology

 

Assistive technology is a very broad term used to describe the technologies whose primary purpose is to aid individuals with disabilities. But to be more specific cognitive assistance primarily focuses on any device or software that enables people to better understand and interact with their surrounding environment.

 

Such as word prediction software which greatly assists people to recall specific words to help improve their linguistic skills. Using specific custom fonts in order to make readability easier.

 

Browsers having a focus mode in order to temporarily disable notifications or other distractions and maybe even enlarging certain portions of text just like when you hover your mouse over this segment.

 

Gene therapy:

 

Gene therapy is a revolutionary approach to treating genetic disorders by manipulating an individual's genetic material to correct, replace, or supplement faulty genes.

 

It involves introducing genetic material into a person's cells to either restore the function of a mutated gene or provide a new function that compensates for the defective gene. The ultimate goal of gene therapy is to treat or cure diseases at their root genetic cause.

 

Accessible gaming controllers for disabled people

 

A vast majority of devices were designed for individuals that can use their arms or legs properly. If an individual is lacking the ability to use their hands then a new set of special devices must be tailored specifically for their use case.

Prosthesis and Implant advancements

 

Advancements in science have finally reached a point where the reconnection of body parts is no longer limited to replacing body parts such as limbs and arms. We have finally reached a monumental milestone due to the fact that a man who had been paralyzed for 12 years due to a cycling accident has regained the ability to walk using a new device that connects his brain and spinal cord.

 

The device, known as a "digital bridge," allows the man to control his legs with his thoughts. Electrodes on his brain transmit signals to electrodes on his spinal cord, stimulating movement.

 

This breakthrough was achieved through a combination of brain and spinal implants, training sessions, and a computer system worn by the patient. The study, published in the journal Nature, demonstrates significant progress in restoring voluntary movement to individuals with spinal cord injuries.

Cuffless Blood Pressure Monitoring Device

Technology is playing a helpful role in monitoring hypertension. People can more readily monitor their blood pressure. The Cuffless blood pressure is one promising development. Instead of traditional cuffs that can be uncomfortable, cuffless monitoring uses modern methods including sensors and algorithms to detect blood pressure without the need for a tight band around the arm. People may monitor their blood pressure using these tools, which are commonly included in smartphones, making it easy and painless for people to check their blood pressure. This technology can simplify and improve the management of hypertension, providing a potentially helpful remedy for people.

Assistive Technology For ADHD

People with ADHD (Attention Deficit Hypertension Disorder) can manage their symptoms as well as improve their quality of life with the use of modern technology, which offers tools and resources to help them do so. Organization, time management, and focus are made easier by apps and digital technologies. While instructional software experience is supported by text-to-speech and speech-to-text technologies. The daily lives of people who have ADHD are improved by wearable technology, meditation apps, and medicine reminder apps. Additionally, online counseling services offer simple access to mental health treatment. Technology gives people with ADHD the ability to better control their symptoms and overall health, with lifestyle changes recommended by healthcare providers.
